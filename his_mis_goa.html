History

World Trade Council of Wichita (formerly the World Trade Club) was organized in 1975/76 by co-founders Dr.Lee Nehrt, Dr.Dharma De Silva & Dean Larry McKibbin at the Chamber with 11 area MNCs to promote international trade & investment in Greater Wichita and South Central Kansas. The WTCouncil originated in 1974 as the International Relations Committee of the Wichita Area Chamber of Commerce. WTCouncil was instrumental in making Wichita a U.S. Customs Port-of-Entry & attracting the Wichita office of the U.S. Department of Commerce, initially housed in the School of Business. WTCouncil assisted in the establishment of the M-A World Trade Center (presently Kansas World Trade Center). Today the Council is affiliated with Wichita State University Barton School's Center for International Business Advancement [CIBA] and the Wichita Metro Chamber. In a unique strategic alliance CIBA-WTCouncil linkage has organized and conducted over 450 country/company trade events and over 110 International Educational seminars. In recognition of our work and ideals to foster international trade the council has own US President's "E" & "E Star" awards for continued export education and service excellence.

Mission and Goals

World Trade Council of Wichita, Inc. [WTC] is organized exclusively as a non-profit organization to foster the work and ideals of international trade via export educational seminars, country & agency trade programs, and company specific international trade/export strategy & success case studies as role models. Specifically, purposes and objectives/goals are designed to:

  • Provide a forum for networking and exchanging professional experiences among members and visiting country Ambassadors, Consuls General, Trade Commissioners, trade agencies, trade delegates and trade specialists;
  • Present country/agency/company trade programs with best market prospects by organizing monthly trade meetings, international trade seminars & annually-nationally proclaimed World Trade Week events;
  • Advocate policy positions on trade issues [e.g., exports, imports, trade barriers] and public policy legislation;
  • Assist in updating Kansas International Trade databank, maintained by CIBA, to conduct periodic surveys to accurately portray “Kansas Export Profile, Problems & Prospects”, and nominate member firms for export achievement awards for export of goods/service excellence;
  • Identify opportunities for trade and cultural missions and encourage participation from member companies, and report Council’s activates to the media;
  • Bring an awareness of International Business [IB] & develop future manpower resources via the annual High School International Trade Essay Prize/Scholarship competition, IB Scholarships and Internships for students enrolled in WSU-IB Majors & IFS-LAS Majors, and offer opportunities for IB students, IBSA/AIESEC to assist in the conduct of WTC country programs;
  • Serve as an advisory group to WSU, Barton School’s Center for International Business Advancement [CIBA] & USED/BIE project, and to develop strategies to further internationalization of business education at universities and industry.
  • To this end, WTC cooperates with CIBA-WSU, USED/BIE Projects, AIESEC/IBSA-WSU, The Chamber, KsWTC, Ks ITCC, federal/state/city agencies, public officials, embassies, consulates general, trade associations which foster, develop and encourage the same purposes/goals.
